FINESSE-Bench is a hierarchical benchmark suite for evaluating financial competencies in LLMs. It combines eight specialized datasets with 3,993 questions spanning foundational finance, exam-style reasoning, technical analysis, derivatives trading, and a Russian-language olympiad block. The suite is designed to measure domain breadth, performance degradation across difficulty levels, and transfer from classical public financial benchmarks to more professional finance tasks.

Methodology

A unified protocol across multiple-choice, numerical-answer, and short-answer financial tasks.

01

Prompting and inference

Fixed prompt templates are used across task types. No few-shot demonstrations are added. Deterministic settings are used where applicable.

02

Scoring

Tasks are evaluated under an LLM-as-judge setup. The article states that GPT-5.2 was used as the judge model for binary correctness scoring.

03

Benchmark structure

The suite supports aggregated reporting over public benchmarks, exam-like tasks, and trading / technical-analysis tasks, as well as per-dataset inspection.

04

Confidence intervals

The paper reports that 95% confidence intervals are computed with bootstrap. This site is wired to display CI overlays as soon as you add them into CI_DATA.
